The premise

Month 1. A contractor slides an envelope across your desk. ₹3 lakh. Monthly. He's held the sanitation contract for 8 years. Refuse, and the garbage trucks stop running. Accept, and you're inside a system you don't yet understand.

This is the first of 60 months. Some months bring crises — a flood, a mafia, a rumour spreading on WhatsApp. Some bring choices — build the road before elections, or the clinic that nobody will remember you for. Most bring both.

Mandate is a simulation built from fictional scenarios, real laws, and real governance dilemmas. There is no clean path. There is only the path you choose — and the ward that lives with it.

How it works
01
Choose your background
The Reformer, the Inheritor, or the Builder. Each starts with different public trust, party standing, and personal wealth. Your background shapes what's easy — not what's possible.
02
Govern actively
Between crises, open the Governance Dashboard to spend on projects. Roads before elections. A sports ground in Year 1. A health clinic nobody photographs. Timing matters as much as intent.
03
Respond to events
12 major decisions across 5 years. Each has delayed consequences — what you do in Year 1 comes back in Year 4. Natural calamities, funding crises, and political tests arrive without warning.
04
Read the law card
After every major decision, the actual law that governs the situation unlocks. RTI Act. AMRUT Mission. 74th Amendment. You remember a law better because you just lived the problem it tried to solve.
05
Face the election
Month 59. Five years of decisions determine your result. The ward you built. The people who stayed or left. The reputation you earned or bought. The result is calculated, not random.
06
See your archetype
The Rising Reformer. The Operator. The Loyalist. The Fallen. Your leadership score and archetype reflect 60 months of choices — shareable as a card that says more about you than you'd expect.

How the score is calculated
Public Approval
62%
How much your ward residents trust and support you. Weighted average across all 7 voter groups. Max: 100.
Party Support
50%
How much your political party values your loyalty and effectiveness. Completely separate from voters. Max: 100.
The party does not care if voters love you. Voters do not care if the party loves you. You need both. This is the central tension of democratic politics. Your combined score adds the two together out of 200.
